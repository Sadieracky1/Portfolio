LM386 Amplifier

The LM386 is a versatile and easy-to-use Class-AB audio power amplifier IC designed for low-voltage, battery-powered consumer applications like radio and portable stereo systems. It features an internally set voltage gain of 20, which can be increased to 200 with an external capacitor and resistor, requires minimal external components, and has a wide operating voltage range from 4V to 18V.

40106 Phase Shift Oscillator

A phase-shift oscillator is a linear electronic oscillator circuit that produces a sine wave output. It consists of an inverting amplifier element such as a transistor or op amp with its output fed back to its input through a phase-shift network consisting of resistors and capacitors in a ladder network. The feedback network 'shifts' the phase of the amplifier output by 180 degrees at the oscillation frequency to give positive feedback.

Triple Vibrati Atari Punk Console

The Triple Vibrati Punk Console is a DIY electronic instrument combining a low-frequency oscillator and an Atari Punk Console-style sound generator to create rich, dynamic sound effects. Using 555 and 556 timer chips, it produces modulated square-wave tones, with three knobs for adjusting pitch and tone, as well as one separate volume knob. The LFO feeds into the sound generator, altering its base frequency for pulsating and warble sound effects.

Twin-T Oscillator

A twin-T oscillator is an RC oscillator that produces a low-distortion sinusoidal output by using an active op-amp to provide positive feedback through a twin-T notch filter. The twin-T filter, a combination of parallel low-pass and high-pass RC networks, creates a null at a specific notch frequency. When placed in the feedback path of an amplifier, this network causes the circuit to oscillate at the notch frequency.

Handmade Kalimba

This is a handcrafted kalimba built using metal prongs mounted on a repurposed cigar box. Each prong’s length was carefully measured to produce a specific pitch, allowing the creation of a complete musical scale across the instrument. By fine-tuning the length of each tine, the kalimba achieves bright, resonant tones when plucked. The cigar box serves as a natural acoustic resonator, amplifying the vibrations and giving the instrument a warm, organic sound. This project combines hands-on craftsmanship, acoustic principles, and musical experimentation to produce a fully playable, custom-tuned instrument.
